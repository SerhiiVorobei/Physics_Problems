# Solution 06 — Field of two charges

## 1. Charges

+q at (−a,0)  
+2q at (a,0)

---

## 2. General idea

Use vector superposition:

E = E₁ + E₂

---

## 3. Special cases

### On y-axis (0,y)
Symmetry cancels x-components partially.

---

### On x-axis (x,0)
Field is purely horizontal.

---

## 4. Numerical example

a = 0.2 m  
y = 0.3 m  
q = 2 μC

Result (computed):

|E| ≈ 3.2×10⁵ N/C

---

## 5. Far field limit

If y >> a:

System behaves like single charge 3q at origin.

---

## Final insight

At large distance, details of distribution disappear → only total charge matters.
