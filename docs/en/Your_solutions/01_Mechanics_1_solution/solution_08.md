# Solution 08 — Circular Motion (Earth rotation)

## 1. Given data

- Earth radius: R = 6378 km = 6.378 × 10⁶ m
- Earth rotation period: T = 24 h = 86400 s

We find centripetal acceleration at the equator.

---

## 2. Angular velocity

ω = 2π / T

ω = 2π / 86400 ≈ 7.27 × 10⁻⁵ rad/s

---

## 3. Centripetal acceleration

a = ω²R

Substitute:

a = (7.27 × 10⁻⁵)² × 6.378 × 10⁶

a ≈ 0.0337 m/s²

---

## 4. Final answer

a ≈ 0.034 m/s²

---

## 5. Interpretation

This acceleration is small compared to gravity (9.81 m/s²), which is why we do not feel Earth's rotation.
However, it slightly reduces effective weight at the equator.
