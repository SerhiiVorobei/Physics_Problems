# Solution 05 — Relative Velocity

## 1. Given data

- River flows east with velocity: v_r = 2 m/s
- Boat speed in still water: v_b = 5 m/s
- River width: d = 200 m
- Desired direction: straight north

---

## 2. Idea of solution

To move straight north, the boat must cancel the eastward current.

So the west component of boat velocity must equal river velocity.

---

## 3. Angle calculation

Let θ be angle west of north.

Horizontal condition:

v_b sinθ = 2

So:

sinθ = 2/5

θ = arcsin(2/5)

θ ≈ 23.6°

---

## 4. Time to cross river

Northward velocity:

v_n = v_b cosθ

v_n = 5 cos(23.6°)

v_n ≈ 5 × 0.917 = 4.59 m/s

Time:

t = d / v_n

t = 200 / 4.59 ≈ 43.6 s

---

## 5. Final answers

- Direction: 23.6° west of north
- Crossing time: ≈ 43.6 s

---

## 6. Physical interpretation

Boat must "aim upstream" to cancel river drift.
Actual motion is straight north due to vector addition of velocities.
